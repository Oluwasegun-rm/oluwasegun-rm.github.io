About

Who Am I?

Oluwasegun Soji-John is a Computer Science student at Morgan State University whose interests span software engineering, artificial intelligence, and data science. He enjoys building intelligent, data-driven systems that solve real-world problems by combining scalable software with machine learning and modern data technologies. He has industry experience developing enterprise software through Oracle's Fusion ERP Financials Development team, where he contributed to backend systems and AI-powered tools that improved enterprise workflows, usability, and operational efficiency.

In addition to his software engineering experience, Oluwasegun conducts AI and machine learning research at Morgan State University's Machine Intelligence and Data Science Lab (MINDS), where he works with large-scale clinical datasets, predictive modeling, and healthcare-focused AI applications. His research has contributed to two peer-reviewed publications, reflecting his commitment to developing practical, data-driven solutions with meaningful impact. Outside of engineering and research, he serves as President of MorganHacks, the largest HBCU hackathon in Maryland, where he leads initiatives that connect students with industry professionals, expand access to technical opportunities, and foster a culture of innovation, collaboration, and continuous learning.

Featured Projects

Featured Projects

Some of my recent research, software engineering, and AI-focused work.

AI-Powered Enterprise Assistant for Oracle ERP

Developed an AI-powered agent during my Oracle internship to simplify how business users interact with Oracle Enterprise Scheduler Service within Oracle Fusion ERP. The assistant allowed users to query job statuses, submit jobs, parse error logs, and receive business-friendly explanations through natural language instead of navigating a complex technical interface.

Highlight: Built a functional AI agent prototype using Oracle AI Agent Studio, Fusion APIs, Python, prompt engineering, and backend tool chaining to improve enterprise workflow usability.

Manifold-Informed Cohort Discovery for CHD Risk Stratification

Contributed to the Manifold-Informed Cohort Discovery framework, a healthcare AI project focused on uncovering hidden high-risk patient groups in imbalanced coronary heart disease data. The framework combined clinically informed feature selection, UMAP manifold learning, and clustering to identify CHD-negative patients with risk profiles similar to confirmed CHD cases.

Highlight: Helped develop a clinically interpretable approach that achieved strong predictive performance with an AUROC of approximately 85.1%, outperforming traditional risk and imbalance-learning baselines.

JobMatch AI Dashboard

Built and deployed an AI-powered resume and job description analysis platform that helps users compare resumes against job postings. The dashboard allows users to upload or paste resume content, analyze job descriptions, view match scores, identify missing skills, and receive AI-generated suggestions through a clean, modern interface.

Highlight: Designed a functional SaaS-style dashboard with resume parsing, match scoring, skill analysis, keyword insights, and a polished user experience for job application support.

Personalized Insulin Dosing with Q-Learning

Worked on an interpretable reinforcement learning framework for personalized insulin dosing in patients with Type 1 Diabetes and thyroid dysfunction. The project used tabular Q-learning to model comorbidity severity, delayed medication effects, and treatment safety in order to support more personalized chronic disease management.

Highlight: The proposed model improved Time-in-Range by 15% and reduced hypoglycemic events by 42% compared to clinical baselines, with expert review showing strong clinical alignment.
Research

Research Interest and Focus

Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare: Developing data-driven AI systems that support clinical decision-making, risk prediction, and personalized care, with a focus on using real-world healthcare data to uncover meaningful patient patterns.

Clinical Data Science and Predictive Modeling: Applying machine learning methods to structured and unstructured clinical datasets to identify disease risk factors, improve prediction accuracy, and support early intervention in healthcare settings.

Knowledge Graphs and Biomedical Data Integration: Building knowledge-driven systems that connect clinical concepts, patient data, and biomedical relationships to make complex healthcare information easier to analyze and interpret.

Personalized Medicine and Reinforcement Learning: Exploring reinforcement learning and decision-support models for personalized treatment recommendation, especially in chronic disease management and patient-specific care planning.

Research Areas

AI in Healthcare and Clinical Decision Support

Designing AI systems that support healthcare analysis, clinical decision-making, and early risk detection using real-world patient data. My work focuses on building practical models that connect technical performance with meaningful clinical interpretation.

Biomedical Knowledge Graphs and Data Integration

Building knowledge graphs that organize and connect clinical data related to diseases such as coronary heart disease. This includes integrating data from multiple sources, structuring unstructured medical text, and creating representations that support downstream analytics and machine learning.

Machine Learning for Risk Stratification

Applying machine learning to identify hidden patient risk groups in imbalanced healthcare datasets. My work includes using clinically informed features, manifold learning, and clustering methods to uncover latent risk signals that may not be captured by traditional classification models.

Reinforcement Learning for Personalized Medicine

Exploring reinforcement learning approaches for personalized treatment recommendation in chronic disease management. This includes modeling patient state, treatment effects, and safety-aware reward functions to support interpretable and patient-specific decision-making.

Healthcare Data Processing and Model Evaluation

Working with real-world healthcare data requires careful preprocessing, validation, and evaluation. My research involves cleaning clinical datasets, handling noisy or incomplete data, analyzing model behavior, and evaluating performance using clinically meaningful metrics.
